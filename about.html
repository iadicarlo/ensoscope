About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Utrecht University working on the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) and how its behaviour and impacts change under a warming climate. My research connects large-ensemble climate simulations and observations to understand how the strongest El Niño and La Niña events, and the rainfall and heat extremes they drive, may shift in the decades ahead.

This platform grew out of that work, in collaboration with Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF). Its aim is practical: to translate the operational seasonal forecasts produced by major centres (ECMWF and Météo-France) into clear, regional signals that humanitarian and health teams can use for anticipatory action, months before a season unfolds. It is a transparent layer on top of world-class forecasts, not a replacement for them, and every method and data source is documented openly on the Methodology page.

What you can do here

  • Forecast - the latest ENSO outlook and the probability of a strong or extreme event in the months ahead.
  • Teleconnections - where past El Niño and La Niña phases shifted rainfall, drought and heat stress.
  • Skill - how trustworthy the forecasts are, by season and lead time, verified against observations.
